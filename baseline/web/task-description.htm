Task Description

Thank you for completing the questionnaire.

During the course of the experiment you will be given 4 queries on different nutritional topics. For each of them you will see some search results. It's your task to provide a credibility rating for the information found on the website without having access to it. Credibility refers to the extend to which the content of a page can be considered to present unbiased, correct and balanced information.
Your credibility judgement will be given on a 11 point scale ranging from "very non credible" to "very credible". Please use the neutral option only if you have no idea what information the website could contain.

Please note that we are only interested in the credibility of information found on the page that would open if you clicked on the search result, not in the general credibility of the whole website.

The search results will look like the example below. They are based on the standard search results you are familiar with from search engines like Google. The search results appear in a random order, which means that there is no relationship between their order and their credibility.
It consists of:

  • the title of the website
  • the url of the website
  • two sentences that were extracted from the page that are representative for the page's content
